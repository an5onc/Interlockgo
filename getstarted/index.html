Get Started: Step‑by‑Step Guide

Everything you need to complete your ignition interlock and finish the DMV requirements — explained.

Follow these steps

Timelines and fees can vary by case. When in doubt, call us — we’ll walk you through it.

1 Confirm your eligibility & hold requirements DMV / Court

Check the reinstatement letter for mandatory items (interlock period, SR‑22, alcohol/therapy classes, reinstatement fees). If you’re unsure, contact the The Colorado Department of Motor Vehicles at 303-205-5600.

  • Schedule an appointment with the DMV to take your written exam. Due to long wait times, it’s best to book early.
  • Schedule an installation appointment with an approved ignition interlock provider at least 72 hours in advance of scheduled written exam, it takes 1-3 business days for the DMV to receive the lease agreement from the provider.

We can help you understand your letter and next steps. Call us at 970-515-5740 or email us.

2 Choose your ignition interlock & schedule install InterlockGo

We install LifeSafer and Guardian devices. These devices are nationally approved and meet all state requirements. Most installs take 2-3 hours.

  • Call: Schedule your installation.
  • Bring: valid ID, vehicle registration, and payment method. We accept cash, credit, and debit. No American Express.
  • Instructional videos are available LifeSafer or Guardian. A one-on-one training session will also be provided before you leave the shop. This ensures you are 100% confident in operating the device.
3 File insurance (SR‑22) with the DMV Insurance

Ask your insurer to submit an SR‑22 to Colorado DMV electronically. Keep it active for the full required period.

  • Request proof of filing for your records.
  • Your insurance company will send proof of insurance directly to the DMV.
4 Complete alcohol education / treatment (if required) Classes

If your letter lists classes or therapy, enroll right away. The DMV requires a certificate of enrollment or completion before issuance of a restricted permit.

  • Ask for electronic submission to speed things up.
  • Proof of enrollment requires one class session completed.
5 Install day Shop Visit

Drive to the rear of building, that is the garage entrance. Installation of the device can take 2-3 hours depending on the year, make, and model of your vehicle.

  • After installation, training can take up to 15 minutes.
  • If you need to fill out your ignition interlock affidavit, we can assist you, and notary services are available.
  • Bring affidavit and any other required documents to the DMV.
  • We will electronically submit your lease agreement to the DMV within 1-3 business days.
  • Depending on your program, you may be responsible for the installation labor fee and any applicable taxes.
  • We accept cash, credit, and debit. No American Express.
6 Apply for your interlock‑restricted license DMV

Once your device is installed, SR‑22 is on file, and you are enrolled or completed with your alcohol classes, visit the DMV to take your written exam for the issuance of your restricted permit.

  • Bring ID and any fees listed on your letter.
  • Confirm the length of your interlock requirement program.
  • Once you are issued your restricted permit, your time for the interlock program will begin. It does not begin the date of your interlock installation.
7 Return for regular calibrations & data uploads Ongoing

Stay on schedule to avoid lockouts. We help with rescheduling, reminders, and reports required by the DMV.

  • Most clients calibrate monthly unless otherwise required.
  • Colorado clients are able to extend their daily interval between calibration appointments to 45 days.
8 Removal & full reinstatement Finish

When your interlock period ends and you’re issued your unrestricted license by the DMV, you are able to schedule a removal appointment.

  • This shop monitors your compliance and will assist with the removal process when that time approaches.

FAQ

How long will I need interlock?

It varies by your case and DMV decision. Unless you are eligible for early re-instatement, the average time is 2 years.

What is an SR‑22?

It’s proof of financial responsibility filed by your insurer with the DMV. It’s not insurance by itself; keep your policy active the entire time.

What do I bring with me to my first appointment with the DMV?

Bring your affidavit of enrollment/completion certificate, ignition interlock affidavit, and reinstatement application.
